India’s Lunar Legacy

Chandrayaan (Lunar Exploration Mission)

The Chandrayaan program, initiated by ISRO,
is India’s ambitious lunar exploration initiative,
aiming to unravel the Moon’s mysteries
and advance space technology.
Spanning from 2008 to 2023,
it has positioned India as a key player in global space research,
with plans for sample return in 2026–27
via Chandrayaan-4.

Chandrayaan-1

Launched on October 22, 2008,
Chandrayaan-1 was India’s first lunar probe,
orbiting the Moon for 312 days.
It made history by confirming water molecules
on the lunar surface,
laying the foundation for future missions.

Chandrayaan-2

Launched on July 22, 2019,
Chandrayaan-2 features an orbiter
that has been operational for over 6 years,
studying the Moon’s surface
with eight advanced instruments.
Despite the lander challenge,
it continues to provide valuable data.

Chandrayaan-3

Launched on July 14, 2023,
Chandrayaan-3 successfully landed on August 23, 2023,
at the lunar south pole,
making India the fourth country
to soft-land on the Moon.
Its 14-day mission expanded our understanding
of the lunar terrain.
